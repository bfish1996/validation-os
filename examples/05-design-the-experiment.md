# 05 · Design the experiment — `/experiment-design`

**You type:** "design an experiment"

▎ Top of the test-next queue: ASM-004, Risk 75. Its Metric for truth is
▎ already countable. Design against it?

Rung and feasibility are weighed upfront, trade named:

▎ Ideal rung: Signed intent (~60) — a priced pilot offer. Feasibility
▎ today: Low — no offer page, price never tested in conversation.
▎ Recommend Anecdotal interviews (~15), High feasibility: 10 warm ICP
▎ owners, past-behaviour questions only. Book Signed intent as the
▎ follow-up once the price survives contact.

Both bars are pre-registered before anything runs — the pass bar carried
forward from the Metric for truth, the kill bar written new:

```markdown
## EXP-003: Do ICP owners already spend £40/mo-equivalent on reconciliation?
- **Assumption**: ASM-004 · **Type**: Anecdotal
- **Feasibility**: High · **Result**: Running · **Date**: 2026-07-14
- **We're right if**: ≥6 of 10 owners report ≥£40/mo cash or ≥4 hrs/mo of
  their own time on reconciliation today.

### We're wrong if
≥5 of 10 report under an hour a month, or describe it as already solved.
```

Two gated writes: create EXP-003, then flip ASM-004 `Status → Testing`.
Prep follows from `references/interview-guide.md` — screener, target N,
non-leading questions ("walk me through your last month-end", never "would
you pay £40?") — into the record body. Then it stops: recruiting and
running the interviews are yours.

**What this shows:** rung choice is strength × feasibility with the trade
named, pass and kill bars are fixed before the test runs, and the skill
ends at a prepared instrument — never an executed one.
