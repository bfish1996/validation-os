# 04 · Find existing evidence — `/find-evidence` (desk)

**You type:** "what does the market say about £40/mo — desk research
ASM-004"

The skill resolves the record, then picks the flavour honestly:

▎ Before searching: "our owners will pay" is a behavioural claim. Desk
▎ research can establish a base rate — what comparable tools charge, what
▎ owners pay bookkeepers — but can't validate the belief; only revealed
▎ behaviour can. I'll log findings as context (Result: Inconclusive), so
▎ Strength stays 0 and Confidence will not move.

The sweep runs under the desk-research harness — sub-questions, source
tiering, triangulation, a refute pass — and comes back with:

▎ Bookkeeper rates £25–35/hr, median SMB reconciliation ~3 hrs/month (two
▎ A-tier sources and one B, agreeing) → owners already pay £75–105/month
▎ for this job. Nearest competing tools: £29–49/month. Refute-pass hit:
▎ two sources note sub-5-employee businesses often do it unpaid, after
▎ hours — the base rate may not hold at the smallest end of the ICP.

One gated write per qualifying piece:

```markdown
## EXP-002: What do ICP owners already pay to reconcile?
- **Assumption**: ASM-004 · **Type**: Desk research
- **Result**: Inconclusive    (base rate ≠ validation)
- **Strength**: 0             <!-- derived; conclusive Results only -->
```

The body carries per-claim provenance. Confidence on ASM-004: unchanged.
Close-out points forward: base rate favourable, the belief still needs
revealed evidence → `/experiment-design`.

**What this shows:** the flavour is chosen by what can actually settle the
Metric for truth, a base rate is logged as context and never as validation,
and every claim lands with provenance.
